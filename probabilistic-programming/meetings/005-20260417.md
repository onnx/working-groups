## Summary

### BaseFlow–ONNX Collaboration Exploration Meeting
Lars joined from the BaseFlow team to explore potential collaboration opportunities with ONNX. Andreas provided an introduction to ONNX and offered additional resources. The discussion focused on early-stage alignment, introductions, and identifying areas for future collaboration between the two frameworks.

### Bayesian Integration in ONNX Framework
The group discussed integrating Bayesian inference into ONNX as a core capability. This includes supporting frameworks such as Stan, PyMC, BaseFlow, Turing.jl, and rxinfer.jl. A major focus is revising the PRNG specification to support MCMC workflows and enable consistent model deployment across environments. Standardizing internal representations was highlighted as a key challenge.

### Specification Document Review Discussion
The team reviewed the current specification document and agreed it needs simplification. Concerns were raised about using terms like “identical,” which may be too strict, especially in floating-point contexts. Alternatives such as “deterministic” or “known seeds” were suggested. The document is still a draft and will undergo further refinement.

### ONNX RNG Specification Revision Discussion
The group discussed revising the ONNX RNG specification to better support Bayesian frameworks. The goal is to simplify the current document into a smaller set of core principles, with additional details deferred. Hans was introduced as a new contributor working on SpaceFlow.

### ONNX Specification for Bayesian Inference
Brian outlined efforts to develop an ONNX specification for Bayesian inference, focusing on portability across environments. This includes PRNG design and future support for posterior inference operators. Existing work, such as PyMC-to-ONNX and Stan-to-Pyro pipelines, may be leveraged.

### ONNX Collaboration Opportunities Discussion
The discussion covered concrete collaboration opportunities, including PRNG specification development and BaseFlow model export to ONNX. Brian outlined working group structures and encouraged contributions, particularly around operator development and interoperability. Next steps include reviewing documentation and scheduling follow-up meetings.

Adam and Brian reviewed the overall structure of the RNG proposal, confirming that the current effort involves updating four existing operators while introducing two new operators. 
The team agreed that the proposal should be circulated for feedback from the Operators SIG before implementation begins. 
Andreas suggested gathering input from execution providers and other ONNX stakeholders once the proposal is publicly available.

The group discussed the need for a formal ONNX roadmap to improve transparency and coordination between ONNX, ONNX Runtime, TensorRT, and related projects. 
Andreas noted that ONNX's recent acceptance into the PyTorch Foundation creates an opportunity to increase project visibility and potentially attract

### RNG Operator Specification Clarification
Brian and Adam reviewed the detailed operator specifications and clarified that the primary behavioral change involves removing 
explicit seed attributes while preserving deterministic PRNG state handling. 
They agreed to simplify documentation language and focus on clearly communicating the required operator updates rather 
than describing implementation details prematurely.

### Collaboration and Outreach Planning
The team reviewed ongoing outreach efforts involving the Stan community, BayesFlow contributors, and the INLA Python project DALIA. 
Brian committed to contacting Bob Carpenter regarding RNG testing methodologies and following up with 
additional probabilistic programming communities that may provide valuable feedback on the proposal.

### Proposal Finalization and Review Process
The meeting concluded with agreement to finalize the RNG proposal over the following days and submit it to the 
ONNX Operators SIG for formal review. After feedback is collected, the team plans to engage execution provider 
maintainers and implementation stakeholders to ensure the proposal is practical to support across ONNX runtimes 
and deployment environments.
